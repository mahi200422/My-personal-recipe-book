Bolani Tareh (Afghan Leek Turnovers)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup water, plus more if needed
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, plus more for cooking
  • 2 cups leeks, white and light green parts only, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Gradually add the water and the tablespoon of vegetable oil,
    mixing until a shaggy dough forms.
  2. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes, or until smooth and elastic. Add
    a little more water if the dough is too dry.
  3. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal balls. Cover them with a damp cloth and let them rest for 15-20 minutes.
  4. While the dough is resting, prepare the filling. In a medium bowl, combine the thinly sliced leeks, chopped cilantro,
    and black pepper. Mix well.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each dough ball into a thin circle (about 6-8 inches in diameter).
  6. Place a spoonful of the leek filling on one half of the dough circle, leaving a small border around the edge.
  7. Fold the other half of the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges firmly to seal, and then
    crimp the edges with a fork to ensure they are completely closed.
  8. Heat some vegetable oil in a large skillet or griddle over medium heat.
  9. Cook the bolani in batches, for 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and slightly crispy. Add more oil to the skillet
    as needed.
  10. Remove the cooked bolani from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  11. Serve the bolani tareh warm. They are often enjoyed with yogurt or a chutney.

Aushak (Afghan Dumplings with Leek Filling)

Ingredients:

  • For the Dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • ¬æ cup water, plus more if needed
  • For the Filling:
    • 4 cups leeks, white and light green parts only, finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon dried mint
    • ¬Ω teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
    • Salt and black pepper to taste
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • For the Yogurt Sauce:
    • 2 cups plain yogurt
    • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon dried mint
    • Salt to taste
  • For the Tomato Topping:
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
    • ¬Ω teaspoon dried mint
    • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. **Prepare the Dough:** In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Gradually add the water,
    mixing until a shaggy dough forms. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes,
    or until smooth and elastic. Add a little more water if the dough is too dry. Divide the dough into 2-3 portions, cover with a damp cloth,
    and let rest for 30 minutes.
  2. **Prepare the Filling:** While the dough rests, heat the vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat.
    Add the finely chopped leeks and cook until they are very soft and translucent, about 15-20 minutes. Stir in
    the dried mint, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and black pepper. Let cool slightly.
  3. **Prepare the Yogurt Sauce:** In a bowl, combine the plain yogurt, minced garlic, dried mint, and salt.
    Mix well and set aside.
  4. **Prepare the Tomato Topping:** Heat the vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion
    and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the diced tomatoes (with their juice),
    tomato paste, dried mint, salt, and black pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  5. **Assemble the Aushak:** On a lightly floured surface, roll out one portion of the dough very thinly into
    a large circle. Using a knife or pizza cutter, cut the circle into 2-inch squares. Place a small spoonful of
    the leek filling in the center of each square. Bring the opposite corners of each square together to form a triangle and pinch the edges to seal.
    You can also bring all four corners together and pinch to seal. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
  6. **Cook the Aushak:** Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Gently drop the aushak into the boiling water,
    a few at a time, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the
    dumplings float to the surface and are cooked through.
  7. **Serve the Aushak:** Using a slotted spoon, remove the cooked aushak from the water and arrange them on a serving platter.
    Spoon the garlic yogurt sauce generously over the dumplings, and then top with the warm tomato topping.
  8. Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious Aushak!

Mango Panna Cotta

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream (halal)
  • 1/2 cup whole milk (halal)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons gelatin powder (halal)
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • For the Mango Puree:
    • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and chopped
    • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (optional, adjust to sweetness of mangoes)
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin powder over the cold water and let it bloom for 5-10 minutes.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and sugar. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally,
    until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is just simmering around the edges. Do not boil.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Add the bloomed gelatin to the warm cream mixture and stir until completely dissolved and smooth.
  5. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl to ensure a smooth texture.
  6. Pour the panna cotta mixture into individual serving glasses or ramekins.
  7. Let them cool slightly at room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight,
    until firm.
  8. **Prepare the Mango Puree:** In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped mangoes, sugar (if using), and lemon juice.
    Blend until smooth. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve for an extra smooth puree.
  9. To serve, spoon the mango puree over the chilled panna cotta. You can garnish with fresh mango slices or mint leaves if desired.

Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • For the Crust:
    • All-purpose flour: 2 cups
    • Cold unsalted butter (from permissible sources), cubed: 1 cup (2 sticks)
    • Granulated sugar: 1/4 cup
    • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon
    • Ice water: 4-6 tablespoons
  • For the Filling:
    • Apples (peeled, cored, and sliced): 6-8 medium
    • Granulated sugar: 1/2 cup (adjust to your preference)
    • All-purpose flour or cornstarch: 2 tablespoons
    • Ground cinnamon: 1 teaspoon
    • Ground nutmeg (optional): 1/4 teaspoon
    • Fresh lemon juice: 1 tablespoon
    • Unsalted butter (from permissible sources), cut into small pieces (optional): 2 tablespoons
  • For the Egg Wash (optional):
    • Egg yolk: 1
    • Milk or water: 1 tablespoon
    • Coarse sugar: for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Make the Crust: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt.
    Cut in the cold butter using a pastry blender or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together.
    Be careful not to overmix.
  2. Divide the dough in half (one slightly larger than the other for the bottom crust).
    Form each half into a disc, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Make the Filling: In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour or
    cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg (if using), and lemon juice.
  4. Assemble the Pie: Preheat oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C).
  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the larger disc of dough to a 12-inch circle.
    Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie plate. Trim the edges, leaving a 1/2-inch overhang.
  6. Pour the apple filling into the pie crust. Dot with small pieces of butter (if using).
  7. Roll out the remaining dough to an 11-inch circle. Place the dough over the filling.
    Trim the edges, leaving a 1-inch overhang. Crimp the top and bottom crust edges together to seal.
    Cut several slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  8. Bake: If desired, brush the top crust with the egg wash (egg yolk mixed with milk or water)
    and sprinkle with coarse sugar.
  9. Bake for 45-60 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. If the crust
    starts to brown too quickly, you can loosely tent the pie with aluminum foil.
  10. Let the pie cool on a wire rack for at least 2-3 hours before slicing and serving.

French Pudding (Pot de Cr√®me)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream (halal)
  • 4 large egg yolks (halal)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional for Chocolate Pot de Cr√®me: 2 ounces halal dark chocolate, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325¬∞F (160¬∞C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and salt until pale and slightly thickened.
  3. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it is just simmering around the edges. Do not boil.
  4. Gradually whisk the warm cream into the egg yolk mixture, a little at a time, to temper the eggs. Stir in
    the vanilla extract (and melted chocolate if making chocolate pot de cr√®me).
  5. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl to ensure a smooth texture.
  6. Pour the custard into small ramekins or custard cups.
  7. Place the ramekins in a baking dish and pour hot water into the dish until it reaches about halfway up
    the sides of the ramekins (this creates a water bath).
  8. Bake for 25-35 minutes, or until the edges of the custard are set but the centers are still slightly jiggly.
  9. Carefully remove the baking dish from the oven and let the ramekins cool in the water bath for about 15-20 minutes.
  10. Transfer the ramekins to the refrigerator and chill for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, until completely set.
  11. Serve chilled. You can garnish with whipped cream (halal), fresh berries, or a dusting of cocoa powder if desired.

French Crepes

Ingredients:

  • All-purpose flour: 1 cup
  • Granulated sugar: 1 tablespoon
  • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Large eggs: 2
  • Milk: 1 1/2 cups
  • Unsalted butter, melted: 2 tablespoons (plus more for cooking)
  • Vanilla extract: 1/2 teaspoon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until just combined. Stir in the melted butter and vanilla extract (if using).
  3. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, whisking constantly until the batter is smooth and lump-free.
  4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours) to allow the batter to rest.
    This helps the gluten relax, resulting in more tender crepes.
  5. Heat a lightly buttered non-stick skillet or crepe pan over medium heat.
  6. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the hot skillet, tilting the pan quickly to spread the batter evenly into a thin circle.
  7. Cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the edges of the crepe are lightly golden and the top appears set.
  8. Carefully flip the crepe with a thin spatula and cook for another 30-60 seconds until lightly golden on the other side.
  9. Transfer the cooked crepe to a plate and keep warm. Repeat with the remaining batter, buttering the pan as needed.
  10. Serve the crepes warm with your favorite fillings, such as fruit, whipped cream, chocolate spread, or powdered sugar.

    • Baking: Preheat oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C). Place the sambosas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush
      the tops with a little vegetable oil or egg wash. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.

Chicken Salad with Balsamic

Ingredients:

  • Cooked chicken breast: 2 cups (shredded or diced)
  • Celery: 1/2 cup (diced)
  • Red onion: 1/4 cup (finely diced)
  • Grapes: 1/2 cup (halved or quartered, if large)
  • Nuts (such as pecans or walnuts): 1/4 cup (roughly chopped) - optional
  • Fresh parsley: 2 tablespoons (chopped)
  • For the Dressing:
    • Mayonnaise: 1/2 cup
    • Balsamic vinegar: 1 tablespoon
    • Mustard: 1 teaspoon
    • Sweetener (such as honey or maple syrup): 1/2 teaspoon (optional)
    • Salt and black pepper: to taste
  • Optional: Lettuce leaves or croissants for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Ensure the chicken is cooked and cooled. Shred or dice it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Combine Ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the shredded or diced chicken, diced celery, finely diced red onion,
    halved or quartered grapes, chopped nuts (if using), and chopped fresh parsley.
  3. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, balsamic vinegar, mustard, and sweetener (if using).
    Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  4. Dress the Salad: Pour the balsamic dressing over the chicken mixture. Stir gently until all the ingredients are well coated.
  5. Chill: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve: Serve the chicken salad on lettuce leaves, in croissants, or on its own.

Lemonade

Ingredients:

  • Fresh lemons: 6-8 (depending on size)
  • Water: 6-8 cups (adjust to taste)
  • Sweetener (such as sugar, honey, agave syrup): 3/4 - 1 cup (adjust to taste)
  • Optional: Mint sprigs, lemon slices, or ice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Wash the lemons thoroughly.
  2. Squeeze the juice from the lemons. You should get about 1 to 1.5 cups of lemon juice. Remove any seeds.
  3. Make a Simple Syrup (optional, but recommended for granulated sugar): In a saucepan, combine the
    sweetener (if using granulated sugar) with 1 cup of water. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sweetener is completely dissolved.
    Remove from heat and let it cool slightly. If using honey or agave, you can skip this step and mix it directly with the lemon juice and water.
  4. In a large pitcher, combine the fresh lemon juice, the simple syrup (if made), and the remaining water.
    Start with 6 cups of water and adjust to your preferred strength.
  5. Stir well to ensure everything is thoroughly mixed.
  6. Taste the lemonade and add more sweetener or water as needed to achieve your desired balance of sweetness
    and tartness.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the lemonade to chill.
  8. Serve cold with ice and garnish with mint sprigs or lemon slices, if desired.

Mallow Flower Syrup

Ingredients:

  • Fresh mallow flowers (Malva sylvestris): 2 cups (loosely packed, petals only)
  • Water: 2 cups
  • Sweetener (such as sugar or honey): 1 cup (adjust to taste)
  • Lemon juice: 1-2 tablespoons (helps preserve color and adds a touch of tartness)

Instructions:

  1. Gently rinse the mallow flowers under cool water to remove any dirt or insects. Pat them dry.
    Use only the petals, removing any green parts.
  2. In a saucepan, bring the water to a boil.
  3. Once boiling, remove the saucepan from the heat and add the mallow flower petals.
  4. Stir gently to ensure all the petals are submerged.
  5. Cover the saucepan and let the flowers steep in the hot water for at least 4-6 hours,
    or preferably overnight, at room temperature.
    This will allow the water to extract the color and flavor from the flowers. The liquid should turn a beautiful shade of blue or purple.
  6. Strain the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a clean saucepan,
    discarding the spent flower petals.
  7. Add the sweetener (sugar or honey) to the strained liquid.
  8. Heat the saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly until the sweetener is completely dissolved.
  9. Once the sweetener is dissolved, bring the syrup to a gentle simmer. Simmer for about 5-10 minutes,
    allowing it to thicken slightly. Be careful not to boil it vigorously.
  10. Stir in the lemon juice. The color of the syrup might change slightly to a more vibrant pink or purple
    depending on the pH.
  11. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mallow flower syrup cool completely.
  12. Pour the cooled syrup into a clean, airtight bottle or jar. Store it in the refrigerator.
    It should last for several weeks.
  13. Use the syrup to flavor drinks, desserts, or other culinary creations. You can mix it with water
    or sparkling water for a refreshing drink.

Cherry Juice

Ingredients:

  • Fresh cherries: 2 pounds (pitted)
  • Water: 1 cup (adjust to desired consistency)
  • Sweetener (such as sugar, honey, or maple syrup): to taste (optional)
  • Lemon juice: 1 tablespoon (optional, to enhance color and flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the fresh cherries thoroughly and remove the pits.
  2. Place the pitted cherries in a saucepan. Add the water. The amount of water will affect the thickness of the juice.
    For a thicker juice, use less water.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat. Once simmering, reduce the heat to low and cook for about 15-20 minutes,
    or until the cherries are soft and have released their juice.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool slightly.
  5. Strain the cherry mixture through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a clean bowl or jar. Use the back of a spoon to press down
    on the solids to extract as much juice as possible. Discard the solids.
  6. Taste the juice and add sweetener (if using) according to your preference. Stir until the sweetener is dissolved.
  7. Stir in the lemon juice (if using).
  8. Let the cherry juice cool completely.
  9. Pour the juice into a clean bottle or container and store it in the refrigerator. It will keep for about 3-4 days.
  10. Serve chilled. You can dilute it with more water or sparkling water if desired.
